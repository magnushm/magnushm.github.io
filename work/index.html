The Scandinavian Institute of Culture, 2018-2020.

The Scandinavian Institute of Culture was me living in a sailboat in various harbors in Oslo, Norway from 2018 to 2020 to not pay rent and do my own thing for a while. I received a small arts grant from Oslo Kommune and bought a boat in order to run an artspace (and to live there) I organized a few shows, none of which I plan to document here. I had a wood stove, a small Opinel knife and a bunch of tools for reparing stuff. The boat was in really bad shape but I got it reasonably cheap and managed to fix it up. In the summer I went swimming every morning, and every day I’d ride my bike to and from my job at the Norwegian National Broadcasting Corporation as a part-time direct transcriber. I met quite a few interesting characters.The boat was a Maxi Fenix 28 ft. sailboat from ca. 1982. Due to not receiving more grants and being broke I sadly had to scrap the boat, though the idea was to donate it to a fairly large and well-known art institutions as a commentary on the lack of general funding for young artists in Oslo. The SIOC is kind-of-alive with the intention of organising shows in the summer of 2022.
